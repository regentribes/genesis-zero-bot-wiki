# Global Manufacturing Capacity Report

**Status:** active
**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Source:** [View source](/genesis-zero-bot-wiki/sources/0010-global-manufacturing-capacity-report.html)

## Key Claims

- Total global manufacturing output: ~$12.3T/yr across 15 sectors
- Global manufacturing workforce: ~1.2 billion persons
- Annual energy consumption: ~220 EJ
- Raw material extraction: ~90 billion tonnes/yr
- The report catalogs all major industrial processes needed to rebuild civilization-scale manufacturing from first principles

## Scope

The report contains six major sections:

1. **Foundational Requirements** — Energy infrastructure (coal, gas, nuclear, solar, wind, hydro, geothermal, biomass), electricity transmission and distribution, water infrastructure, industrial gases
2. **Material Extraction & Primary Processing** — Iron/steel, aluminum, copper, nickel, cobalt, lithium, rare earths
3. **Chemical Manufacturing** — Sulfuric acid, ammonia, fertilizers, chlor-alkali
4. **Materials Processing & Manufacturing** — Cement, glass, ceramics, refractories
5. **Polymer & Plastics Manufacturing** — Polyolefins, polyesters, polyamides, PVC, polystyrene, polycarbonate
6. **Energy-Intensive Unit Operations** — Distillation, membrane separation, adsorption

## Relevance to Regenerative Communities

This document serves as the canonical engineering reference for community-scale bootstrap planning. It provides:

- First-principles resource budgets (energy, water, materials per unit output)
- Supply chain dependencies between industrial sectors
- Capacity data needed to size community-scale infrastructure
- Technical specifications for procurement and manufacturing planning
