## Summary

Elinor Ostrom's 8 principles for governing commons successfully:

1. Clearly defined boundaries (who belongs, what resources)
2. Proportional equivalence of benefits and costs
3. Collective-choice arrangements (most affected participate in rules)
4. Monitoring (monitors accountable to community)
5. Graduated sanctions (small violations handled locally)
6. Conflict-resolution mechanisms (cheap and fast)
7. Recognition of right to self-govern (not challenged by external authorities)
8. Nested enterprises (multiple layers of governance for larger systems)

These principles emerged from studying thousands of real-world commons that succeeded without privatization or state control.
