# Autocatalysis

Self-sustaining chemical reaction networks in which the products of a reaction catalyze the reaction itself, creating feedback loops that drive the system toward complex self-organization. In the context of algorithmic chemistry (Ben Goertzel), autocatalytic sets are proposed as a substrate for emergent cognition — where patterns reinforce themselves through self-catalyzing loops rather than top-down instruction.

In regenerative systems thinking, autocatalysis maps to any positive feedback loop where a system's outputs become its own inputs — compost producing heat that accelerates decomposition, community knowledge compounding through shared practice, or mutual aid networks that grow denser as they are used.

**Key claim (from Goertzel):** Autocatalytic thought patterns function as self-reinforcing belief systems and may have more creative power than abduction or evolutionary learning alone.
